HISTORICAL MILESTONES IN TECHNOLOGY


1999

Invention of At-Home Wi-Fi

Wi-Fi was first invented in 1997 when a group called 802.11 was also invented. Soon, IEEE802.11, a set of standards which define wireless communication for local networks, was created. After this, the structure of Wi-Fi was made, allowing the transfer of two megabytes per second of data between devices. Finally, in 1999, at-home use was created almost two years after the original invention. Wi-Fi today is used in millions of homes across America and many people couldn't live without it. It is outstanding how much one invention can affect the lives of millions within only 20 years. Wireless connection to the internet simplifies the way we use technology daily. It can be found in homes, stores, restaurants, and just about anywhere you go in America. It is the definition of streamline technology in this day and age.


2000

Release of the First USB Flash Drive

The USB flash drive was first patented in 1999 by a company called M-Systems. However, the first one wasn’t sold until late 2000 in the U.S. by I.B.M. The prices of USB drives have dramatically dropped since they were first produced. In 2000, they were 30 dollars for 128 megabytes, but now, they are around 10 dollars for 8 gigabytes. This trend should continue as technology develops. They are extremely helpful in the field of technology, considering their small size and large storage. Without USB flash drives, it would be much more difficult to share documents and downloads across devices because flash drives make this process a lot quicker. They can also be used for storage when a device is full and you need to transfer files to a new device. Overall, the invention of the USB flash drive was surprisingly recent, but very effective.


Release of the First Camera Phone

The very first camera phone was made in June 2000. It was released in South Korea by Samsung, named the SCH-V200. This phone flipped open and revealed a 1.5-inch TFT-LCD (a built in camera) and it could take only 20 pictures, each one with a resolution of 350,000 pixels (or 0.35 megapixels). It also had to be connected to a computer in order to view the pictures because it was technically a separate camera in the same device as the phone. Although this was the first phone to be able to take pictures, the first phone that was capable of sending pictures digitally was the J-SHO4 released by J-phone in Japan. The resolution on this phone was 110,000 pixels (or 0.11 megapixels), which was significantly less than the SCH-V200, but the sharing abilities made up for the lower photo quality. The camera phone made its first appearance in America in November 2002, and since then, it has grown to become one of the most used features in cell phones.


2007

Release of the First iPhone

The first generation iPhone, release on June 29, 2007, was very different than the iPhones today. It had a screen size of 4.5 inches by 2.4 inches, no third-party apps, and 16GB of memory. It also only ran on AT&T which, at the time, was extremely slow. The introduction of the iPhone also meant the introduction of the first touch screen. Phone companies before had only released phones with physical keyboards and smaller screens. Believe it or not, people believed the iPhone wouldn’t receive any significant market share because of the high prices and little profit. The CEO of Microsoft even claimed this; but later in 2016, he admitted to being wrong and stated that Microsoft should’ve followed Apple’s footsteps. The release of the iPhone amazed customers across the U.S. and it set a much higher standard for any future cell phone company who would compete against one of the biggest brands in America.


2010

Invention of Apple's Retina Display

Retina Display was made by Apple in 2012 and its name comes from Apple’s statement that the screens with Retina Display are so high quality that the human eye could not possibly see the individual pixels. The technology smooths the edges of the pixels to make them much less obvious on the screen. It also includes high contrast to make the colors lighter and darker depending on where they are placed. In-plane switching technology is another factor that comes into Retina Display and it makes it easier for the screen to be viewed at several different angles. The overall quality of the iPhone screen was improved after this release. The chemically-treated glass and the LED backlighting play a big part in making better quality images for the human eye. Against popular belief, screen resolutions do not make up Retina Display entirely. The two main parts of Retina Display are pixel density and distance from the user’s eye to the screen. This invention drastically changed cell phone quality for the better.
